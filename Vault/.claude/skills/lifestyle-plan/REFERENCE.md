# Lifestyle Plan — Reference

---

## INTAKE PROTOCOL

Run all questions before building any plan. Do not assume any answers. Do not pre-load constraints.

1. Current weight and height?
2. Target weight and timeline? (soft or hard deadline?)
3. Age?
4. Current activity level — sedentary / light / moderate / active?
5. Any injuries, physical limitations, or medical conditions affecting exercise?
6. What does a typical day of eating look like now?
7. Any foods you genuinely enjoy and want to keep?
8. Any foods you can't stand or won't eat?
9. How many days/week can you commit to exercise? What time of day?
10. Any non-negotiable lifestyle preferences? (e.g. one treat meal/week, no meal prep, etc.)

Store all intake answers at the top of the plan file. Reference them for all future sessions.

## EATING FRAMEWORK

### Structure
- 3 main meals + 1–2 optional snacks
- Protein at every meal (satiety + muscle preservation during weight loss)
- No food banned. Portions and frequency are the levers.
- Calorie range: calibrate to user's weight, activity level, and goal pace from intake.

### Principles
- Protein: prioritise at every meal — eggs, chicken, fish, dairy, legumes
- Veg: fill half the plate with vegetables at main meals
- Carbs: not banned — timed and portioned relative to activity
- Fats: portion-controlled — calorie-dense, include regularly in small amounts

### Foods to reduce (not ban)
- Liquid calories (soft drink, juice, alcohol) — biggest hidden source
- Ultra-processed snack foods — easy to overeat, low satiety
- Large portions of refined carbs without protein alongside

## EXERCISE PROGRESSION

### Phase 1 — Foundation
**Goal:** Build the habit. Not the fitness.
- Low-intensity cardio (walking preferred). 3–4 sessions/week. Start short — build duration.
- No gym required.
- Track: did I go? How long? How did I feel?

### Phase 2 — Build
**Goal:** Increase duration and introduce light resistance.
- Cardio 4–5x/week. Build toward 45–60 min sessions.
- Add 2x/week bodyweight resistance: lower body + core. Avoid anything contraindicated by injury intake.

### Phase 3 — Intensify
**Goal:** Add load and variety. Maintain injury safety.
- Continue cardio or replace with gym sessions.
- Compound lower body: goblet squat, Romanian deadlift, leg press.
- Upper body: based on injury clearance from intake.

## INJURY PROTOCOL

**If intake reveals an injury:**
- Ask: what is cleared? What is not cleared? Has physio given specific guidance?
- Build the exercise plan around confirmed clearances only.
- Flag any exercise that may aggravate the injury.
- Never assume clearance. Never push through pain.

**If shoulder injury:**
- Avoid: overhead press, lateral raises, pull-ups/chin-ups, upright rows.
- Safe alternatives: horizontal push (chest press), horizontal pull (cable rows), core work.

**If knee injury:**
- Avoid: deep squats, lunges, high-impact cardio.
- Safe alternatives: cycling, swimming, step-ups (small range), leg press (partial range).
