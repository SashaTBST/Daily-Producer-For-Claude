# Tax Return — Reference

## FY2025 Rate Card

### Income Tax Brackets (residents, Stage 3 cuts applied from 1 July 2024)

| Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 – $18,200 | 0% (tax-free threshold) |
| $18,201 – $45,000 | 19c per $1 over $18,200 |
| $45,001 – $135,000 | 32.5c per $1 over $45,000 |
| $135,001 – $190,000 | 37c per $1 over $135,000 |
| $190,001+ | 45c per $1 over $190,000 |

Plus 2% Medicare levy on all taxable income (standard rate).

### Low Income Tax Offset (LITO) — FY2025
- Max $700 at income ≤ $37,500
- Phases out at 5c per $1 from $37,500–$45,000
- Then 1.5c per $1 from $45,001–$66,667
- Nil above $66,667
- Auto-applied by ATO — no action needed

### LMITO
Ended FY2022–23. Not available for FY2025.

### Medicare Levy Surcharge — no private hospital cover

| Single income | Family income | Surcharge |
|---|---|---|
| $93,001 – $108,000 | $186,001 – $216,000 | 1.0% |
| $108,001 – $144,000 | $216,001 – $288,000 | 1.25% |
| $144,001+ | $288,001+ | 1.5% |

Family threshold increases by $1,500 per dependent child.
Surcharge applies to taxable income + total net investment losses + reportable fringe benefits.

### Working From Home — Fixed Rate Method (FY2025)
- 70c per hour worked from home
- Covers: electricity, gas, internet, phone, stationery, minor equipment
- Does NOT cover: depreciation of dedicated office furniture or equipment (claim separately)
- Requires: actual record of hours (diary or log) for the full year — sample/estimate not accepted from FY2023 onward

### Lodgment Deadlines — FY2025
- Self-lodge via myTax: **31 October 2025** (PASSED)
- Registered tax agent: **15 May 2026** (33 days from 2026-04-12)

## D-Items — Work-Related Deduction Categories

| Code | Category | Key Rules |
|---|---|---|
| D1 | Work-related car expenses | Cents per km (88c/km FY2025, max 5,000km) OR logbook. Commute home→work not claimable. |
| D2 | Work-related travel | Overnight work travel. Meals + accommodation. Not daily commute. |
| D3 | Clothing/laundry | Protective gear, occupation-specific uniform, or compulsory uniform only. No plain clothing. |
| D4 | Self-education | Course directly connected to current role. Not for new career path. |
| D5 | Other work-related | Home office (WFH), phone/internet (work %), tools, equipment, industry subscriptions, professional memberships |
| D6 | Low-value pool | Assets <$1,000 pooled for depreciation |
| D7 | Interest deductions | Loan interest on income-producing investments |
| D8 | Dividend deductions | Costs of managing share/fund investments |
| D9 | Gifts and donations | DGR-registered charities only, $2 minimum, receipt required |
| D10 | Cost of managing tax affairs | Prior year's tax agent fees only (current year fees claimed next year) |

**$300 rule:** Work-related expenses total under $300 (excluding car, travel, clothing, self-education) — no receipt required, but must explain the basis for the claim.

**Mixed use:** Phone, internet, home office — must apportion work vs personal. Document the method.

## CGT Rules

### General
- CGT event = disposal: sale, trade, gift, or using asset to purchase goods/services
- Capital gain = proceeds minus cost base
- Cost base includes: purchase price + brokerage/transaction fees + any costs of improvement
- **50% CGT discount**: applies when asset held 12+ months before disposal. Halves the net capital gain.
- Capital losses: must offset capital gains in the same FY first. Net losses carried forward — cannot offset against ordinary income.
- Net capital gain (after discount and losses) added to taxable income.

### Crypto — Specific Rules
- Every crypto disposal is a CGT event: selling to AUD, trading crypto-to-crypto, spending crypto, gifting crypto
- Cost base in AUD: market value at time of acquisition (plus exchange fees)
- Proceeds in AUD: market value at time of disposal (plus exchange fees)
- 50% discount applies if held 12+ months — check acquisition date on Coinbase report
- Staking rewards = ordinary income at AUD market value on the date received
- Airdrops = ordinary income if received as payment; otherwise CGT asset at $0 cost base
- Record keeping: Coinbase tax report covers all events on-platform — download at Coinbase > Taxes > Reports

### Vanguard Index Funds
- Annual Tax Statement (usually available August–September) contains:
  - Franked and unfranked dividends
  - Franking credits (offset against tax payable — enter exact amount from statement)
  - Capital gains distributions from the fund (discounted and non-discounted — enter as shown)
  - Foreign income and foreign tax offsets (if applicable)
- Unit sales: calculate gain/loss using unit price at acquisition vs disposal, from Vanguard transaction history
- Franking credits: enter the exact amount from the statement — the ATO auto-matches this against fund reports

## Sole Trader — Key Rules

- Net profit (income minus deductible expenses) flows into personal taxable income at marginal rate
- No separate "small business" tax rate for sole traders
- **GST registered:** Income and expenses recorded GST-exclusive (net of GST claimed via BAS)
- **Not GST registered:** Amounts are GST-inclusive — no GST to separate
- Home office: choose fixed rate (70c/hr) or actual cost — cannot split between methods in same FY
- Vehicle: cents per km (88c/km) or logbook — choose one, apply consistently
- **Instant asset write-off (FY2025):** $20,000 per asset for small business (aggregated turnover <$10M) — threshold legislated until 30 June 2026. Flag all asset purchases to tax agent.
- Prepaid expenses: deductible if the service period is 12 months or less and falls within the FY
- Business losses: if sole trader loss, it may be quarantined (non-commercial loss rules) — flag to agent

## Personal Super Contributions (Concessional)

- Deductible as a personal super contribution if:
  1. Contributed to super fund before **30 June** of the FY, AND
  2. **Notice of Intent to Claim Deduction** form lodged with super fund before tax return is filed (or before super fund processes a rollover/payout)
- Annual concessional cap: **$30,000 FY2025** — includes employer (SG) contributions
- Check: employer contributions + voluntary contributions must not exceed $30,000 combined
- Carry-forward unused cap: available if total super balance < $500,000 at 30 June of prior year — check ATO myGov for unused cap amounts
- Fund then sends a tax file number notification; 15% contributions tax applies within super

## Common Flags — Prompt Tax Agent to Review

- Income protection insurance premiums paid personally (not through super) — generally deductible
- HECS/HELP debt — compulsory repayment calculated on taxable income, separate to tax
- Prior year capital losses carried forward — offset against this year's gains
- Foreign income — all foreign source income must be declared
- Spouse/partner offset — may apply if partner income is low
- Depreciation schedule — if work or business assets were purchased, depreciation schedule needed
- Rental income/negative gearing — if applicable (out of scope for current skill setup)
