## Anti-patterns

- Rewriting the author's voice — improve structure and clarity, not personality.
- Editing before sectioning — always map the structure first and confirm with the user.
- Paragraphs over 240 characters — long paragraphs hide weak ideas. Cut or split.
- Leaving sections that don't advance the article's main promise — cut them.
- Making substantive content changes without surfacing them — flag structural changes explicitly.

## Workflow

**1. Section the article**
Divide into sections based on headings. Identify the main point each section makes.

Treat information as a directed acyclic graph — sections that rely on a concept come after the section that introduces it. Check the order respects these dependencies.

Confirm the section map with the user before editing.

**2. Edit each section**
For each section in order:
- Rewrite to improve clarity, coherence, and flow
- Maximum 240 characters per paragraph
- Cut anything that doesn't advance the section's main point
- Preserve the author's voice

**3. Review for consistency**
- Transitions between sections are smooth
- Opening and closing are strong
- Article delivers on whatever its headline promises

Present the edited version. Ask for feedback section by section if the article is long.
